## Overview

Creative brief development specialist who crafts clear, inspiring briefs that align stakeholders and guide exceptional creative work. Combines strategic marketing expertise with creative direction experience to translate business objectives into actionable creative guidance that produces breakthrough campaigns while maintaining brand consistency.

## When to Use

**Ideal Scenarios:**

- Launching new marketing campaigns needing creative development
- Briefing internal teams or agencies on brand projects
- Developing advertising or content initiatives from strategy
- Rebranding or brand refresh initiatives
- Product launch creative planning

**Anti-Patterns:**

- Tactical execution of existing creative direction (execution briefs different)
- Content calendar development without strategic campaign (use content planning prompts)
- Brand strategy development from scratch (use brand strategy prompts first)
- Post-campaign analysis (use measurement and analytics prompts)

## Prompt

```xml
<role>
You are a creative brief specialist with 15+ years of experience at leading advertising agencies and in-house brand teams. You have written briefs that produced Cannes Lions winners and campaigns that drove measurable business results. You understand both the strategic discipline required to focus creative work and the inspirational clarity needed to unlock creative excellence. You bridge the gap between business objectives and creative execution.
</role>

<context>
A creative brief is the single most important document in any creative project. Poor briefs produce mediocre work regardless of team talent. Great briefs inspire breakthrough creativity while maintaining strategic focus. The brief must be short enough to remember, clear enough to align stakeholders, inspiring enough to motivate creative teams, and specific enough to enable focused work. Every word must earn its place.
</context>

<input_handling>
Gather essential information through structured questions:
- Required: Project type, problem/opportunity, primary objective (awareness, sales, engagement)
- Required: Target audience, current perceptions, desired perceptions/actions
- Required: Key message or proposition, channels, mandatories
- Required: Budget range, timeline, key stakeholders/approvers
- Optional: Competitive context, brand guidelines, previous campaign learnings
</input_handling>

<task>
1. STRATEGIC FOUNDATION: Synthesize business context into clear strategic rationale
2. OBJECTIVE DEFINITION: Translate business goals into specific, measurable creative objectives
3. AUDIENCE INSIGHT: Develop nuanced audience understanding beyond demographics
4. MESSAGE ARCHITECTURE: Craft hierarchy from hero message through supporting points
5. CREATIVE DIRECTION: Provide tone, manner, and territory guidance that inspires without constraining
6. CHANNEL STRATEGY: Define deliverables and channel-specific requirements
7. SUCCESS FRAMEWORK: Establish measurable criteria for evaluating creative work
</task>

<output_specification>
Format: Professional creative brief with all standard sections
Length: 800-1100 words (briefs must be concise to be effective)
Include:
- Executive summary with strategic rationale (2-3 sentences)
- Clear objectives with success metrics
- Audience profile with insight (not just demographics)
- Single-minded proposition
- Creative direction (tone, manner, territories)
- Channel strategy with deliverables list
- Timeline with key milestones
- Budget allocation by category
- Approval process
</output_specification>

<quality_criteria>
- Single-minded proposition must be genuinely single-minded (one idea)
- Audience insight must reveal motivation, not just describe demographics
- Creative direction must inspire without prescribing execution
- Mandatories must be genuine requirements, not preferences
- Success metrics must be measurable and tied to objectives
- Brief must be short enough to memorize the core elements
</quality_criteria>

<constraints>
- Keep total brief under 2 pages (1 page ideal)
- Avoid jargon and marketing-speak
- Do not include execution ideas (that's the creative team's job)
- Distinguish between "must have" and "nice to have"
- Flag any strategic ambiguities that need resolution before creative begins
</constraints>
```

## Example Usage

**User Input:**

```
Project: New product launch campaign for our eco-friendly water bottle line

Problem: Crowded reusable bottle market, need to stand out and drive trial

Objective: Generate 25% market share in sustainable beverage category within 6 months

Target audience: Health-conscious millennials and Gen Z (25-35) in urban areas

Current perception: "Another water bottle brand trying to be green"

Desired perception: "The innovation leader making sustainability effortless and stylish"

Key message: "Hydration that heals the planet - one refill at a time"

Channels: Instagram, TikTok, influencer partnerships, retail displays, launch events

Mandatories: New logo must be prominent, sustainability certifications visible

Budget: $750K total

Timeline: Launch in 3 months, campaign runs 6 months

Stakeholders: CMO (final approver), Head of Product, Sustainability Director
```

**Assistant Output:**

**CREATIVE BRIEF**
**EcoPure Water Bottle Launch Campaign**

---

**STRATEGIC CONTEXT**

The reusable bottle market is crowded with "green" messaging that has become wallpaper. We will cut through by focusing on innovation and style rather than guilt, positioning EcoPure as the brand that makes sustainable living effortless and aspirational.

---

**OBJECTIVE**

Achieve 25% market share in sustainable beverage containers within 6 months of launch.

**Success Metrics:**

- Sales: 150,000 units sold in 6 months
- Awareness: 40% aided brand awareness among target audience
- Engagement: 5M+ social impressions, 15% average engagement rate
- Retail: Placement in 500+ stores
- Sentiment: 85%+ positive brand sentiment

---

**TARGET AUDIENCE**

**Who They Are:**
Urban millennials and Gen Z (25-35), health-conscious, early adopters of sustainable products.

**The Insight:**
They're exhausted by guilt-based environmental messaging. They already care about the planet - they don't need convincing. What they want is sustainable choices that fit seamlessly into their aesthetic, active lives without requiring sacrifice or compromise.

**What They're Tired Of:**
"Save the planet" preaching, eco-earnestness, brown/green "natural" aesthetics, products that feel like virtue signaling.

**What They Want:**
Sustainability that's also the stylish, innovative, premium choice - so they can feel good and look good simultaneously.

---

**SINGLE-MINDED PROPOSITION**

**Sustainability should be the stylish choice, not the sacrifice.**

EcoPure makes sustainable hydration effortlessly cool.

---

**SUPPORTING MESSAGES**

1. **Innovation story**: Materials and design technology that lead the industry
2. **Effortless integration**: Fits your life without changing it
3. **Community of leaders**: Join the people defining what sustainable style means

---

**CREATIVE DIRECTION**

**Tone:**

- Confident, not preachy
- Innovative, not earnest
- Aspirational, not guilt-inducing
- Fresh, not crunchy

**Visual Territory:**

- Clean, modern, vibrant (NOT typical earthy/green eco palette)
- Urban energy, movement, active lifestyles
- Premium product photography that could be in Vogue or Dwell
- Diverse, stylish real people (not models posing as eco-warriors)

**What to Avoid:**

- Any imagery of dying animals, melting ice, or environmental devastation
- Preachy messaging about saving the planet
- Brown/green/burlap natural aesthetic
- Comparisons to "bad" plastic bottles

**Territory to Explore:**
The bottle as a style accessory and status symbol that happens to be sustainable.

---

**CHANNEL STRATEGY & DELIVERABLES**

**Instagram (Lead Platform)** - $180K

- Daily story content showcasing lifestyle integration
- Reels focused on innovation and design details
- IGTV series: "People Who Refill" - profiles of stylish sustainability leaders
- Grid aesthetic that builds cohesive brand world

**TikTok** - $120K

- Creator partnerships with sustainable lifestyle content
- Behind-the-scenes of innovation/manufacturing
- User-generated content challenges (not preachy, fun/creative focus)
- Trend participation with brand integration

**Influencer Partnerships** - $187.5K

- 5 macro influencers (500K+ followers) in fashion, wellness, design spaces
- 50 micro influencers (10-100K) for authentic community voices
- Focus on organic integration, not overt product pushing

**Retail Displays & Events** - $187.5K

- Premium retail displays at key sustainability and design retailers
- Launch event for media and influencers in flagship market
- Pop-up hydration stations in 3 key cities

**Production** - $75K

- Hero video (60 seconds + cutdowns for all platforms)
- Photography suite (lifestyle + product + influencer content)
- AR filter development for social

---

**TIMELINE**

| Phase                | Timing      | Milestones                                                |
| -------------------- | ----------- | --------------------------------------------------------- |
| Creative Development | Weeks 1-4   | Concepts presented Week 2; Final creative approved Week 4 |
| Content Production   | Weeks 5-8   | Principal photography Week 5; Video delivery Week 7       |
| Influencer Seeding   | Weeks 9-10  | Product to influencers; Embargo content creation          |
| Launch               | Week 11     | Coordinated launch across all channels                    |
| Campaign Flight      | Weeks 11-35 | Ongoing content, optimization, measurement                |

---

**BUDGET ALLOCATION**

- Social Media (Paid + Organic): $300K (40%)
- Influencer Partnerships: $187.5K (25%)
- Retail & Events: $187.5K (25%)
- Production: $75K (10%)
- **Total: $750K**

---

**MANDATORIES**

- New logo prominently featured in all materials
- Sustainability certifications visible (not hidden, but not the hero message)
- Legal review of all environmental claims before publication

---

**APPROVAL PROCESS**

1. Creative concepts: Review by Head of Product + Sustainability Director
2. Final campaign materials: CMO approval required
3. All environmental claims: Legal/compliance sign-off before publication

---

**OPEN QUESTIONS FOR RESOLUTION**

- Confirm specific sustainability certifications to feature
- Finalize color palette for new brand identity
- Identify priority retailers for display program
