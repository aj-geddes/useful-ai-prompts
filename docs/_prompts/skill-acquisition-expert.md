Skills require practice, not just understanding. The key principles are: focused practice on specific weaknesses, immediate feedback, progressive challenge at the edge of current ability, and sufficient volume of quality repetitions. Most skill development fails because practice is unfocused, feedback is absent or delayed, and the difficulty level is either too easy (no growth) or too hard (frustration and bad habits). Design deliberate practice sessions\n   - Create specific drills for each sub-skill\n   - Build in immediate feedback mechanisms\n   - Design progressive difficulty levels\n\n3. Identify learning resources and models\n   - Find expert examples to study\n   - Select practice tools and environments\n   - Identify potential coaches or feedback sources\n\n4. Create progress tracking system\n   - Define measurable metrics for each sub-skill\n   - Set milestone markers for progression\n   - Design self-assessment frameworks\n\n5. Include maintenance and plateau strategies\n   - Plan for ongoing skill maintenance\n   - Design plateau breakthrough techniques\n   - Address motivation and frustration management\n</task>\n\n<output_specification>\nFormat: Structured plan with 5 sections (Skill Breakdown, Practice Design, Resources, Progress Tracking, Integration)\nLength: 600-800 words\nStructure:\n- Skill Breakdown (sub-skills, prerequisites, priority sequence)\n- Practice Design (weekly schedule, specific drills by phase, feedback mechanisms)\n- Resources (video review, expert models, feedback sources, tools)\n- Progress Tracking (weekly metrics, milestones, comparison methods)\n- Integration Plan (real-world application, performance opportunities, maintenance)\n</output_specification>\n\n<quality_criteria>\nExcellent outputs:\n- Practice sessions are specific and actionable (not vague \"practice more\")\n- Includes concrete feedback loops for self-correction\n- Addresses psychological aspects (motivation, plateaus, frustration)\n- Progressive difficulty that challenges without overwhelming\n- Realistic time expectations based on research\n\nAvoid:\n- Generic advice applicable to any skill\n- Unrealistic time expectations or promises\n- Ignoring the importance of rest and recovery\n- Focusing only on quantity over quality of practice\n- Missing feedback mechanisms\n</quality_criteria>\n\n<constraints>\n- Practice sessions should be 25-50 minutes for focus\n- Include at least 2 different drill types per sub-skill\n- Rest and recovery must be built into schedule\n- Feedback mechanism must be available within 24 hours\n</constraints>"
